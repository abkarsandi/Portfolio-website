I built a Python model of a recuperated industrial gas turbine and performed a second-law (exergy) assessment. The solver couples mass/energy/exergy balances with temperature-dependent properties to predict net power and thermal efficiency. Below, I compare my model results against the catalog across ambient temperature.

Methodology

Components (compressor, combustor, turbine, recuperator) are modeled with adiabatic balances, realistic isentropic efficiencies and pressure losses. Nonlinear equations are solved iteratively; the working fluid uses temperature-dependent specific heats. Ambient temperature is swept while other conditions are held at the catalog reference. Model and catalog are shown side-by-side for quick visual comparison (identical axes/scales).

Lessons Learned

  • Side-by-side plots make trend comparison immediate; offsets point to component map or loss assumptions.
  • Temperature-dependent properties and recuperator effectiveness dominate off-design accuracy.
  • Digitized catalog curves enable quick error checks without changing the modeling workflow.
