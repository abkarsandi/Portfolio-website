4-Position Kinematic Synthesis (Four-Bar)

Four precision positions were given for a moving body. I computed Burmester circle and center point curves, compared analytical (Excel/Python) and graphical (GeoGebra) solutions, selected feasible fixed and moving pivots, and synthesized a four-bar that passes through all four positions with acceptable transmission angles and link proportions.

Overview

The workflow followed a standard precision-position synthesis: find pole triangles, choose an opposite-pole quadrilateral, generate the Burmester circle-point locus (candidate moving pivots) and the corresponding center-point locus (candidate fixed pivots), then choose a consistent pair to realize the motion and verify the transmission angle and link-length ratio constraints.

Burmester Curves (Analytical vs. Graphical)

The two approaches agree on feasible pivot regions. After comparing alternatives, I selected a pair of Burmester points that satisfied the design constraints and yielded a practical linkage layout.

Synthesis Result

Using the chosen circle/center points, the four-bar was dimensioned and simulated. The mechanism visits all four positions while maintaining transmission angles > 10° and avoiding extreme link ratios (< 10:1). The Excel model and GeoGebra simulation produced consistent motion; the analytical solution required a coordinate shift to match the graphical reference frame, which was corrected in post-processing.

Lessons Learned

  • Analytical and graphical Burmester constructions are complementary—using both helps validate pivot regions.
  • Frame conventions (origin/axes) matter: aligning references prevents apparent “shifts” in the loci.
  • Screening solutions with transmission angle and link-ratio criteria early saves time in iteration.
