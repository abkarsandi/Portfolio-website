Mechanical designer focused on the quadcopter chassis and production drawings. The frame uses carbon-fiber plates and tubes for stiffness/weight and 3D-printed components for mounts and serviceability. We manufactured the airframe, assembled the electronics, and completed successful flight tests.

What I Did

I designed the quadcopter chassis around CF plates and round tubes, created 2D manufacturing drawings for the carbon parts (hole patterns, tolerances, and stack-ups), and developed plastic accessories to simplify mounting and maintenance. Design decisions were driven by stiffness, vibration isolation, and the ability to swap electronics without disassembling the frame.

Chassis & Parts I Designed

The layout places mass centrally to keep the center of gravity near the geometric center. Printed parts act as interface pieces (FC/companion computer trays, wire guides, landing legs), while CF plates carry the loads and CF tubes provide bending stiffness on the arms.

Manufacturing & Assembly

All electronics were mounted with access in mind: power distribution, ESCs, and flight controller can be removed without disassembling the arms. The design keeps wiring paths short and protects connectors during landings.

Flight Test

First flights confirmed structural stiffness and motor alignment; the service-friendly layout made tuning and part swaps quick.

Lessons Learned

  • Design for maintenance: electronics should be swappable without tearing down the frame.
  • Place the center of gravity at the geometric center for predictable control response.
  • Verify motor-mount flatness and orientation to minimize vibration and yaw bias.
